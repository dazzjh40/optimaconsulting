Abandoned Vehicles

Many councils continue to manage abandoned vehicles through slow, multi step processes that delay action, increase workload and limit financial recovery.

Traditional approaches rely on repeated visits, delayed DVLA responses and contractor led removal, resulting in inefficiency and limited control over outcomes.

Typical Challenges

  • Multiple site visits for a single case
  • Delays obtaining keeper details
  • Limited enforcement options when ownership is disputed
  • Contractor led models with minimal financial return
  • High officer time per vehicle removed

Operational Redesign

Drawing on recent local authority experience, we redesigned the abandoned vehicle process to significantly reduce officer time and improve outcomes.

Process Improvements

  • Use of DVLA devolved powers to remove delay
  • Single visit case handling in most scenarios
  • Streamlined decision making and enforcement workflow
  • Improved identification and classification of vehicles

Commercial Improvements

  • Revised contract arrangements
  • Council retains scrap or resale value
  • Reduced reliance on contractor controlled outcomes
  • Improved financial return and cost control

Results

  • Reduced processing time per case
  • Fewer site visits required
  • Improved response times to the public
  • Greater control over enforcement outcomes
  • Enhanced value recovery from vehicles

This approach has been shared with multiple authorities facing similar challenges, particularly where legacy processes and contract arrangements limit flexibility.

If your current process is resource intensive or slow to respond, we can review and redesign your approach to improve efficiency and outcomes.
